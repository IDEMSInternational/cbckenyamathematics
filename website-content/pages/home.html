Supporting Grade 10 Teachers Under the CBC

The Grade 10 CBC Mathematics Project provides curriculum-aligned Open Educational Resources covering the full Grade 10 mathematics syllabus in Kenya. Resources are structured around official learning objectives and developed in accordance with the CBC curriculum framework.

Teachers can access interactive and printable textbooks, lesson plans, teacher guides, and certified professional development courses that support continuous professional growth.

While the current focus is Grade 10, the project is designed to expand to additional grades over time. All materials are openly licensed and developed through collaboration between Kenyan mathematics educators and institutions, enabling ongoing refinement and shared ownership.

These resources are organised into three complementary strands:

Textbooks

A textbook covering the full Grade 10 CBC mathematics curriculum. The recommended format is an interactive online textbook with carefully designed questions that provide immediate, targeted feedback to students. Printable and offline versions are also available.

Access Textbooks

Training courses

A set of certified training courses designed to support teachers in delivering Competency-Based Mathematics education, and in reviewing, contributing to, and strengthening the resources.

Access CPD Courses

Lesson plans, guides, and courses

Full lesson plans and step-by-step guides for each topic in the curriculum, aligned to the learning objectives. Corresponding certified courses support collaborative improvement and professional engagement.

Developed jointly by INNODEMS, the Kenya Mathematical Society (KMS), and IDEMS International

This project is designed as a collaborative, evolving platform. Teachers are encouraged to engage through both the certified CPD courses and the topic-based collaborative courses, where participation in professional discussion and shared reflection contributes directly to the ongoing development of the resources. Active contributors will be recognised in the Contributors section of the project.
