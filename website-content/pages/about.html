Project Overview

This page provides an overview of the Grade 10 CBE Mathematics Project, including its institutional collaboration, contributors, technologies, and opportunities for participation. It outlines how the project is developed, how educators and other collaborators can engage with it, and how the resources are expected to expand in the future.

Institutional collaboration

The Grade 10 CBE Mathematics Project is developed through collaboration between Kenyan institutions, educators, academics, and international partners. The project brings together expertise in mathematics education, teacher professional development, and open educational technologies to create and maintain Open Educational Resources that support teachers implementing the Kenyan Competency-Based Curriculum for mathematics.

INNODEMS leads the development of the educational resources and coordinates collaboration with teachers and subject specialists involved in the project.

The Kenya Mathematical Society (KMS) supports professional engagement with the mathematics community and contributes to the development and certification of professional learning activities for teachers.

IDEMS International provides technical expertise and supports the development of the digital infrastructure used to create, deliver, and maintain the project’s open educational resources.

How to contribute

The ongoing development and improvement of the project’s resources rely on collaboration between teachers, educators, academics, and technical contributors. The initiative is designed as an open and collaborative effort, and individuals interested in supporting its development are encouraged to participate.

Educators can engage through certified CPD courses that support professional development while exploring the use of the project’s resources in the classroom. The project also offers certified topic-based collaborative courses where participants share classroom experiences, discuss teaching approaches, and contribute ideas that help refine and expand the resources. These courses are accessible through the course links provided on this site.

Contributions may include sharing teaching experiences, suggesting improvements to materials, participating in professional discussions, or contributing to the development of new resources. Individuals who play an active role in the project may be formally acknowledged in the Contributors section above.

Technologies used

The project relies on an ecosystem of open technologies used to develop, publish, and support the use of its educational resources.

PreTeXt

An open-source authoring system designed for the creation of technical and educational documents, particularly in mathematics and the sciences. It allows authors to produce high-quality textbooks and learning materials that can be published in multiple formats, including interactive web versions and printable documents.

STACK

An open-source system for creating automatically assessed mathematics questions. It enables students to enter mathematical expressions and receive immediate feedback, supporting learning through interactive problem solving.

Moodle

An open-source learning management system widely used in education. In this project it is used to deliver certified courses and to host collaborative forums where educators discuss teaching approaches, share experiences, and contribute to the ongoing development of the resources.

In this project, the core resources are developed as open digital textbooks using PreTeXt. Interactive mathematics questions created with STACK are embedded within the textbooks through the integration developed between the two systems. Moodle courses allow educators to engage with the materials, participate in professional discussions, and contribute feedback that supports the ongoing improvement of the textbooks and related resources.

Project scope and future expansion

The current focus of the project is the development of resources supporting Grade 10 mathematics within the Kenyan Competency-Based Curriculum.

Draft textbooks for Grades 7–9 have already been developed as part of earlier work. Individuals interested in learning more about these materials are encouraged to contact the project leaders.

Development of Grade 11 resources is expected to begin in the coming months, with Grade 12 materials planned to follow as the project continues to expand.

The technologies and collaborative approach developed for this project may also support the development of open educational resources in other subjects, particularly within STEM disciplines. Institutions and individuals interested in exploring such opportunities are encouraged to contact the project leaders.
